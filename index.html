Claim

The mechanism of the mystery black box contains a siphon and a trap for water.

Evidence

The mystery black box is a mechanism that outputs a seemingly random amount of water based on some input of water. It can be observed that identical inputs may still produce varying outputs. Initially, an input of 500 mL of water produced 0 mL of water, though, for other trials with the same input, the box may also output a drastically greater amount of water. Due to these differences in outputs, we considered that a siphon and trap were significant components of the box. For trials in which the input exceeds the output, a trap can be used to collect the water without dispensing much of the water; for trials in which the output exceeds the input, a siphon can be used to pull extra water from the trap out of the box. In our model, there are pipes of different sizes to act as both the siphons and traps connected to buckets to collect and disperse the water. After testing our model, the outputs generally were the same as our inputs: in one trial, an input of 300 mL of water resulted in an output of 0 mL of water, though in the next trial, an input of 475 mL of water resulted in around 475 mL of water (the same amount).

Reasoning

Models are demonstrations of a system of ideas and can be represented physically (exhibits, structures) or digitally (slideshows, CAD models). Models are very useful for closely demonstrating the structure and purpose of a real or undeveloped idea, especially if analyzing the real idea is difficult. Models allow for easier analysis of an idea, though they are generally simpler than the actual object and may not produce accurate results.

Since for some trials, the output exceeded the input and for some trials, the input exceeded the output, it can be determined that in some cases, water is being collected and prevented from exiting the box (input exceeds output) and that in some cases, extra water is exiting the box, presumably by being pulled from the box (output exceeds input). For example, if we observe Trials 1 and 4 in the model data, it can be seen that input of 300 mL outputs a far less amount of 0 mL, and an input of 500 mL outputted a smaller amount of 400 mL; these trials can relatively be compared with Trials 1 and 18 in the class data, in which an input of 500 mL outputted 0 mL, and an input of 500 mL outputted 155 mL. Additionally, if we observe Trials 5 and 6, an input of 650 mL outputted 650 mL, and an input of 700 mL outputted 700 mL, which occurred due to the siphon extracting water from the collection out of the box (and trapping additional water). These two phenomena describe the effects of a trap and siphon, respectively, as a trap collects and prevents water from escaping the box while a siphon extracts extra water from a collection of water and out of the box.

Suppose the box did not contain either a siphon or a trap. In order to replicate the overall class data of the box, the model needs to match the unique trial results in which the output drastically exceeds the input or the input drastically exceeds the output (examples include Trial 1 and Trial 18 for the first case and Trial 5 and Trial 17 for the second case). Without either a trap or a siphon, these cases should not occur without leaking or adding extra water, and the input should closely match the output without variances: no water can be trapped in the box to prevent water from escaping (since there is no trap), and no extra water should be escaping the box (as there is no siphon).
